Lung Cancer Information

Comprehensive information about lung cancer, symptoms, and treatment options

üîç What is Lung Cancer?

Lung cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the lungs. It occurs when cells in the lung begin to grow uncontrollably, forming tumors that can interfere with lung function.

  • Most common cause of cancer death worldwide
  • Two main types: Non-small cell and Small cell
  • Often linked to smoking but can affect non-smokers
  • Early detection improves treatment outcomes

‚ö†Ô∏è Symptoms to Watch For

Early lung cancer may not cause symptoms, but as it progresses, various signs may appear:

  • Persistent cough that doesn't go away
  • Coughing up blood or rust-colored sputum
  • Chest pain that worsens with breathing
  • Shortness of breath
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Recurring respiratory infections

üéØ Risk Factors

Several factors can increase the risk of developing lung cancer:

  • Smoking (leading cause)
  • Secondhand smoke exposure
  • Radon gas exposure
  • Asbestos exposure
  • Family history of lung cancer
  • Air pollution
  • Previous radiation therapy

üìã Stages of Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is staged to determine the extent of the disease:

  • Stage I: Cancer is localized to the lung
  • Stage II: Cancer has spread to nearby lymph nodes
  • Stage III: Cancer has spread to lymph nodes in the chest
  • Stage IV: Cancer has spread to other parts of the body

üè• Treatment Options

Treatment depends on the type, stage, and overall health:

  • Surgery: Removal of tumor and surrounding tissue
  • Chemotherapy: Drugs to destroy cancer cells
  • Radiation Therapy: High-energy rays to kill cancer cells
  • Targeted Therapy: Drugs that target specific cancer features
  • Immunotherapy: Helps immune system fight cancer

üõ°Ô∏è Prevention Tips

While not all lung cancers can be prevented, you can reduce your risk:

  • Don't smoke or quit if you do
  • Avoid secondhand smoke
  • Test your home for radon
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in fruits and vegetables
  • Exercise regularly
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Get regular check-ups
